Welcome to London

The centre of the human world

London is an incredible city to visit and explore, offering a unique blend of rich history and modern vibrancy. As the capital of the world, it boasts iconic landmarks like Big Ben, the London Eye, and Tower Bridge, which I never tire of seeing.

LONDON EYE

The London Eye is a giant observation wheel located on the South Bank of the River Thames. Standing at 135 meters tall, it offers stunning views of London's skyline, including landmarks like Big Ben, St. Paul's Cathedral, and Tower Bridge. Opened in 2000, it's now one of the city's most iconic attractions, providing visitors with a 30-minute ride in spacious glass capsules for a panoramic experience.

Frameless

WOW O WOW!

Frameless is an immersive digital art gallery in London, located near Marble Arch. It showcases iconic works from artists like Van Gogh and Monet in a completely new way, using floor-to-ceiling projections and interactive displays. The gallery transforms classic art into a multi-sensory experience, blending light, sound, and movement to engage visitors in an unforgettable visual journey.

Greenwich Observatory

The Royal Observatory Greenwich, established in 1675, is famous for its role in the history of astronomy and navigation. It’s the location of the Prime Meridian, the zero degrees longitude line that divides the eastern and western hemispheres. Visitors can explore its historic telescopes, learn about timekeeping, and enjoy stunning views of London from the observatory grounds.

But Who Cares About that!

What I like about the Royal Observatory Greenwich also hosts the Planetarium, where visitors can enjoy immersive shows that explore various astronomical phenomena. The shows feature stunning visuals and fascinating narratives about the universe, stars, and planets. You can see everything from the formation of galaxies to the night sky over London, enhancing the overall visit to this historic site!

Now time for the markets!

Camden Market, a melting pot of music, fashion, experiences and food across four quarters

Market Halls Oxford Street A hidden gem just off Oxford St. Hop on the escalator (also known as the stairway to food heaven) to discover the street food answer to Aladdin’s cave.

Borough Market (FOR THE FOODLOVERS) is one of London’s oldest and largest food markets, with over 100 delicatessens, butchers, fishmongers, and cheesemakers selling fresh produce, meats, artisanal delicacies, and more.

Old Spitalfields Market is home to a community of both life-long traders and exciting new brands. Explore a weekly line up of daily, vintage, vinyl and one off markets, with splendid food to boast.

Brick Lane food. If your an indian/asian food lover like me, then this area is famed for its brilliant curry houses and Indian restaurants and has alot more to offer.

My favourite restaurants in London

The Great Chase

The Great Chase is an award winning restaurant located in the heart of London, with both the owner and staff passionate about delivering high-quality British cuisine with an elegant twist.

Their breakfast sets, alongside their signature dry-aged Angus sirloin, are a must-try. Don’t miss out on their fab tiramisu, sticky toffee pudding and other delightful desserts.

Oliver's Fish & Chips

Oliver’s Fish & Chips in London is a family-run spot known for its traditional, fresh approach to British fish and chips.

With classic haddock and cod meals, alongside homemade sides, are a must-try, especially when paired with their unique take on mushy peas and tartar sauce. Oliver’s also offers great dessert options to round out the authentic experience.

Siirgista Bros

Siirgista Bros, once known as Burgista Bros, is a go-to for London burger lovers. They focus on using high-quality, British-sourced meats for their juicy burgers, paired with fresh toppings and house-made sauces.

Their BBQ burger are a standout, spicy buffalo and BBQ wings, are crowd favorites. Though they’ve rebranded, the food remains just as satisfying, making them a must-visit for gourmet burger fans.

Now on google maps it will say all locations permenantly closed but they are open at North Finchely N12 9QD 726 High Road.

London's PFC
